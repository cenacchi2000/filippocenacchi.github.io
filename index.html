Filippo Cenacchi

PhD Candidate, Computer Science (Frontier AI Research Centre) — Macquarie University, Sydney

My research is at the intersection of humanoid robots, multimodal AI, and mental‑health diagnostics. I design simulation‑first pipelines, trust‑calibrated interaction policies, and privacy‑preserving avatarization to make early assessment more reliable and equitable. I collaborate across computing, psychology, and clinical partners.

Google Scholar GitHub LinkedIn Email Sydney, Australia

Research Focus

Humanoid‑AI Diagnostic Agents

Designing conversational humanoid agents (e.g., Ameca) that sense and fuse facial, vocal, and linguistic cues to support early detection of depression, PTSD, and cognitive decline. Emphasis on trust calibration, safety, and clinician‑style pacing.

Ameca Multimodal Fusion Trust & Uncertainty

Simulation‑First Training

SimClinician: a simulation testbed with MetaHuman patients, synchronized speech/face/gaze and PHQ‑8 / PCL‑C flows. Enables counterfactual replay, nonverbal perturbations, and uncertainty‑aware turn management, reducing hardware wear and accelerating iteration.

Unreal Engine 5 MetaHuman Counterfactual Replay

Privacy‑Preserving Avatarization

Transforming raw sessions into re‑playable 3D avatars that encode behavioral/affective signals while removing identifiers. Supports longitudinal tracking and AI‑assisted querying without storing voices or faces.

3D Avatars Longitudinal Analysis Ethics by Design

Decision Support & Triage

Interactive triage wizards and RL‑inspired probing layered over static datasets (E‑DAIC, DAIC‑WOZ) to select targeted questions that maximize diagnostic clarity while exposing confidence and evidence to clinicians.

E‑DAIC / DAIC‑WOZ RL Probing Explainability
